Image Scanning Microscopy platform. Combination with Fluorescence Lifetime and Stimulated Emission Depletion Imaging Summary Design and implementation of a versatile Image Scanning Microscopy (ISM) platform based on SPAD array detectors, extending conventional confocal microscopy with enhanced spatial resolution, time-resolved fluorescence, and adaptive super-resolution capabilities. The technology is patented and licensed to Nikon, and implemented in commercial microscopy systems.
Role System architecture and implementation, including optical design, detector integration, custom acquisition electronics, real-time control software, and data analysis pipelines. Work carried out in collaboration with Dr. Marco Castello. Technical Highlights
  • Development of an ISM imaging platform leveraging both the spatial and temporal distributions of fluorescence emission, sampled by a SPAD array detector and a custom-made acquisition architecture.
  • Design of a digital frequency-domain DAQ module capable of recording essential spatial and temporal photon features, enabling time-correlated acquisition without conventional TCSPC bottlenecks.
  • Implementation of ISM reconstruction pipelines improving spatial resolution and signal-to-noise relative to standard confocal microscopy.
  • Extension to fluorescence lifetime imaging (ISM-FLIM), enabling simultaneous super-resolution and functional contrast.
  • Support for multi-color and multispecies imaging, including time-domain encoding of fluorophore excitation spectra for robust spectral discrimination.
Related Developments within the Platform
  • STED-ISM: Enhancement of a STED microscope with a detector array, enabling ISM-based detection to achieve a target resolution with reduced depletion intensity, improving photostability and suitability for live imaging.
  • Focus-ISM: Development of a strategy to improve optical sectioning and background rejection in ISM-based techniques, with or without a STED beam, particularly beneficial for thick and scattering samples.
Both approaches require minimal architectural changes to conventional microscopes while providing substantial gains in resolution, contrast, and imaging robustness. Outcome & Impact
  • The ISM technology developed within this project is protected by patents and licensed to Nikon, where it has been implemented in commercial microscopy platforms.
  • Enabled a unified imaging architecture supporting confocal, ISM, ISM-FLIM, ISM-STED, and multispecies imaging modalities.
  • Provided practical advantages for live-cell functional imaging, combining resolution enhancement, functional contrast, and reduced phototoxicity.
Smart, real-Time Single-Particle Tracking with Time-Resolved Detection Summary Conceptualization and implementation of a real-time single-particle tracking (SPT) platform based on a confocal laser scanning microscope enhanced with an asynchronous SPAD array detector. The system integrates spatial localization, fluorescence lifetime measurement, and FPGA-driven feedback control to achieve high-precision, high-speed 3D particle tracking in living cells.
Role Project conception and system architecture. Design and construction of a preliminary working platform, including detector integration, FPGA-based real-time feedback control, scanning synchronization, and custom acquisition and analysis software. Technical Highlights
  • Enhancement of a confocal laser scanning microscope with an asynchronous read-out SPAD array detector, capturing the spatial distribution of emission photons.
  • Real-time localization of the particle position within the excitation volume based on detector array information.
  • Implementation of a closed-loop feedback system, leveraging FPGA-based control to dynamically adjust the scanning mechanism and maintain the particle centered within the excitation focus.
  • Combination of single-particle tracking with fluorescence lifetime measurement, enabled by the independent single-photon detection capability of each SPAD pixel.
  • Achieved 40 nm lateral and 60 nm axial localization precision with 100 detected photons and sub-millisecond temporal sampling for real-time tracking. Offline refinement enabling microsecond-scale temporal precision.
Related Developments within the Platform
  • Validation of spatiotemporal performance by tracking fluorescent beads with diffusion coefficients up to 10 μm²/s.
  • Application to live-cell imaging, tracking lysosome dynamics in SK-N-BE cells.
  • Simultaneous measurement of fluorescence lifetime of membrane-associated markers during particle tracking.
  • Establishment of a framework for future correlative imaging and dynamic functional studies, combining motion analysis with molecular contrast.
Outcome & Impact
  • Demonstrated a robust architecture for integrating real-time localization, lifetime measurement, and adaptive scanning within a unified platform.
  • Advanced the capability of confocal microscopy from passive observation to active, feedback-driven tracking.
  • Provided a scalable foundation for next-generation smart microscopy systems integrating motion control, functional imaging, and computational refinement.

Role Design and implementation of the prototype imaging and tracking platform. Integration of optical components, beam-shaping elements, scanning control, and position reconstruction algorithms. Development of acquisition and tracking software for real-time localization and adaptive coordinate pattern execution. Technical Highlights
  • Extension of the MINFLUX concept toward parallel tracking, moving beyond single-emitter operation to multiplexed configurations within the same field of view.

  • Replacement of single-element detection with camera-based detection, enabling spatial discrimination of multiple emitters and scalable tracking architectures.
  • Development of real-time position estimation algorithms to compute emitter coordinates from structured illumination patterns.
  • Implementation of a control framework coordinatingexcitation pattern updates, excitation pattern updates, scanning mechanisms, localization computation, region-of-interest management
